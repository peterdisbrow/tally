# Social Copy Template

## X / Twitter (under 280 chars)
[Hook or key takeaway] + link

Example: "Your church stream dropped mid-sermon again? Here are 10 fixes that actually work → [link]"

Rules:
- Lead with the pain point or benefit
- No hashtag spam (1-2 max if relevant)
- Include the link at the end
- Skip emojis unless they add clarity

---

## Facebook / LinkedIn (150-300 words)
[2-3 sentence hook restating the problem]

[1-2 sentences on what the post covers]

[Key takeaway or surprising insight from the post]

[CTA with link]

Example:
"Most church streaming problems have nothing to do with your camera or encoder — they start with the network.

We put together a guide covering the 10 most common streaming issues we see churches deal with, plus the fixes that actually work (not the generic 'restart your router' advice).

The one that surprises most teams: audio drift. Your stream looks fine but viewers hear a half-second delay that makes everything feel off. The fix takes 30 seconds.

Read the full guide → [link]"

Rules:
- Open with a counterintuitive or surprising take
- Include one specific detail from the post
- End with a clear CTA and link
- No corporate tone — write like you would in a Slack message to a peer

## Email Teaser

**Subject:** [Short, curiosity-driven, under 50 chars]
**Preview text:** [Completes the subject line, under 90 chars]

**Body (3-5 sentences):**
[State the problem]
[What the post covers]
[One specific takeaway to hook them]
[CTA link]

Example:
Subject: Your stream dropped again?
Preview: Here are 10 fixes that actually work.

Body:
"Nothing kills Sunday morning momentum like a stream crash during the sermon.

We wrote up the 10 most common church streaming problems and the exact fixes for each one — from encoder settings to audio drift to the network config mistake almost every church makes.

The one most teams miss: their switch is set to auto-negotiate instead of forcing 1Gbps full duplex. Takes 30 seconds to fix.

Read the full guide → [link]"

Rules:
- Subject line creates curiosity or states a pain point
- Preview text completes the thought (not just repeating the subject)
- Body is scannable — short paragraphs, one idea per sentence
- One link, at the end
